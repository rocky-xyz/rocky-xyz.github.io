Polynomial Operations and The Decimal System

1. Mathematical Modeling

In a decimal system, any two-digit number can be represented as a first-order polynomial where x=10:

N = 10a + b, P(x) = ax + b

Where a is the tens-place coefficient and b is the units-place coefficient.

2. Polynomial Expansion

When calculating the square of this number, it is equivalent to the binomial expansion of the polynomial:

P(x)^2 = (ax + b)^2 = a^2x^2 + 2abx + b^2

3. Carrying Logic and Coefficient Mapping

In pure algebra, the coefficients a^2, 2ab, and b^2 are independent. However, in decimal arithmetic, if a coefficient exceeds the base 10, a carrying operation is triggered.
